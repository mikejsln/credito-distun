# Crédito Distun — Venture Thesis

**Studio:** InnovArise (Alloy Partners × Grupo AG) · **Stage:** Discover → Build (AI Pass 1 complete) · **Date:** 2026-06-28
**Domain:** Industrial Distribution Channel Gaps · **Lens:** Asset-first (AG purchase data as the underwriting moat)

> **One-liner:** Embedded net-terms + working-capital credit for thin-file ferreterías and small/mid contractors, underwritten at the moment they order from Grupo AG's distribution arm **Distun** — *"compra hoy, paga cuando cobras"* (buy today, pay when you get paid).

---

## The problem
The most expensive, frequent job-to-be-done in the building-materials channel is **financing the gap between buying materials and getting paid for the job.** Banks won't underwrite thin-file ferreterías and contractors (no bureau file); distributors extend terms by gut feel, capping order size and tying up their own balance sheet. Cash dependency throttles both the buyer's growth and AG's basket size.

## The product
A credit layer embedded directly in the Distun order flow (counter, app, or sales-rep tablet): one-tap **net-30 (free, merchant-funded) / net-60–90 (buyer-funded fee)** financing, underwritten on **AG's own transaction history** rather than a bureau file. Balance-sheet-light: originate, then sell/securitize receivables (factoring is codified in Guatemala — Decreto 1-2018). Expand to a stored-value wallet + card.

## The advantage (named, and why it transfers)
1. **Proprietary underwriting data** — AG's purchase history across Distun retail + B2B accounts *is* the credit model: repayment-correlated behavior banks can't see. The venture owns the model + data feed (transfers to an external CEO).
2. **Captive channel = day-one loan book + zero CAC** — AG already extends informal terms; Crédito Distun *formalizes and prices* an existing relationship rather than acquiring strangers.
3. **Geographic whitespace** — funded analogs (R2, Tul, Kontempo, Addi) cluster in MX/CO/BR/CL; none operate at scale in Guatemala/Central America.
> Transferability: AG is **anchor customer + data partner + first warehouse lender, not the sole lender.** The long-term venture is the **credit rail for *any* LatAm building-materials distributor** — which is what makes it venture-scale, not an AG subsidiary. (Strategic note: ship under a standalone brand — e.g. *Cuadra* — with "powered by Distun" as the Guatemala trust mark, to keep the multi-distributor platform story intact.)

## Why now (catalyst)
- Guatemala digital payments ≈ **US$12.7B (2025)** → ~$18.2B by 2028 (Statista) — rails for at-point-of-order credit now exist.
- Fintech base grew **5 → 31 companies (+416%) in 5 years**, concentrated in digital credit (Trade.gov, 2025).
- **Regulatory lane is open *now* and formalizing soon:** lending own/warehouse-funded capital needs **no SIB license** (the reserved activity is deposit-taking); factoring is codified (Decreto 1-2018); **commercial interest rates are uncapped** (Art. 42, Decreto 19-2002). A closing window to build the book + data moat before the perimeter expands (IDB-funded modernization underway; data-protection bill presented Aug 2025).

## Scale (≥$500M floor — clears on expansion, not the wedge)
Guatemala wedge SOM ≈ **$48M originated/yr → ~$2.7M net revenue** — a *proof-of-underwriting* market, not the prize. Expansion stacks: (1) **contractors >> stores** (10–20× the count), (2) **geography** GT → MX → Andean (regional channel ~600k stores / **~$120B** TAM, Tul/BusinessWire 2022), (3) **wallet + card** (credit book → payments take-rate). Long-term obtainable net-revenue pool ≈ **$3.9B/yr**; a 3–5% share = $120–200M revenue → clears the $500M enterprise-value bar at LatAm fintech-lending multiples.

## Unit economics (per loan, base case: $5K order, net-60)
Gross fee 3.0% ($150) − cost of capital (~$73 @ 14% warehouse) − expected loss (~$30 @ 0.6%/cycle, secured) − servicing (~$15) = **~$32 contribution (~64% of gross fee).** Net interest-and-fee margin ~**9–11%** of deployed capital/yr (capital recycles ~6×/yr). **Brutally sensitive to loss rate** — 0.6%→2.0%/cycle halves contribution. The moat must keep loss near the *secured trade-finance* end (Marco: $254M financed, ~0 loss) not the *unsecured SME* end (Mercado Crédito ~6.8% NPL).

## Capital structure (the #1 risk, addressed head-on)
**Equity funds the company; a debt warehouse funds the book** (mirrors Slope's $175M debt / $77M equity). GT wedge needs a **~$10–15M warehouse**; regional scale $80–150M+. **AG/Distun is the natural anchor of the first warehouse** — a strategic distributor lending into its own channel. Originate-to-distribute (sell receivables to a forward-flow buyer) keeps the balance sheet light; the venture earns spread + servicing. *Flag: the standard ~$1M studio Seed under-capitalizes a lending business — pair it with a larger Seed+ and a committed AG-anchored warehouse.*
