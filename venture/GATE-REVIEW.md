# Crédito Distun — Build Gate Verdict
Date: 2026-06-28   Pass: 1 (AI)   Decision: **NEED-MORE-EVIDENCE** (hold; Board-overridable)
Run through: vc-partner (adversarial) + bar-raiser (ambition) + financial-analyst + market-researcher + competitive-funding-intel + regulatory-policy-analyst + risk-diligence-officer · studio-director owns the call.

## Assumptions tested / what we learned (signal vs evidence)
- **Market real & reachable** — *signal.* Thin-file ferretería/contractor credit gap is real and sourced; LatAm SME trade-finance gap ~$350B (Marco). Guatemala wedge is small (~800 ferreterías) by design.
- **Regulatory lane open** — *evidence.* No SIB license to lend own/warehouse funds; factoring codified (Decreto 1-2018); rates uncapped (Art. 42, Decreto 19-2002). The strongest, most defensible claim in the venture.
- **Novelty / defensibility** — *signal.* Reversed prior NEGATIVE → **MIXED-POSITIVE**: Slope (US, ~$252M raised) proves the mechanism is fundable but owns none of the moat (LatAm + Distun channel + AG data). No funded player owns the distributor-captive, proprietary-data-underwritten, Central-America wedge.
- **Unit economics** — *signal (modeled).* ~64% of gross fee retained as contribution *if* loss stays near the secured benchmark (<1%/cycle). Unproven on real repayment data.

## Filter check
- **Commercial Viability:** PASS on the expansion thesis (clears ≥$500M); the wedge alone does not — the gate question is "is MX + contractors + card executable?", not "is Guatemala big enough" (it isn't).
- **5x ceiling:** PASS (path: ~$250M+/yr originations @ 4× revenue exit → studio ~16% post-dilution clears 5x). Down case (GT-only) misses → expansion is the thesis, not optional.
- **Advantage:** PASS-conditional — real and *potentially* transferable, but depends on (a) the data predicting repayment and (b) durable, change-of-control-surviving data/channel contracts.
- **Transferability:** the crux. Moat must transfer to a standalone CEO or this is an AG feature, not a venture.

## Red flags
- **Regulatory line (regulatory-policy-analyst):** GT lane favorable; **must-resolve** = local banking-law opinion (no-license lending + not an inadvertent "financial group") + clean GDPR-lite consent/data chain (no comprehensive GT data-protection law yet; bill presented Aug 2025). Future *card* phase inherits the Ley de Tarjetas rate cap — Phase 3, not launch.
- **Diligence line (risk-diligence-officer):** verdict **CLEAR WITH CONDITIONS.** Top-3 risks: (1) **transferability of the moat**, (2) **model validity** (purchase data → repayment is assumed, not proven), (3) **correlated credit + funding fragility** (single-vertical construction-cyclical book on a thin warehouse — defaults and warehouse pull-back arrive in the same downturn).
- **Capital intensity:** standard ~$1M Seed under-capitalizes a lending business — pair with Seed+ and an AG-anchored $10–15M warehouse.

## Most dangerous untested assumption
**That AG's proprietary purchase history keeps net loss near the *secured trade-finance* benchmark (~0.6%/cycle), not the *unsecured SME* benchmark (~1.5–2%/cycle).** The entire moat and the entire unit economic live or die here, and there is no live loss curve yet.

## Decision: NEED-MORE-EVIDENCE
A real-world evidence gap exists that AI cannot fill: whether AG purchase behavior predicts repayment. Hold at the Build gate pending the cheapest test that resolves it.

### The evidence ask (cheapest experiment, ~$0–5K, no code/license/warehouse first)
**Retro-underwrite AG/Distun's existing 24–36 months of accounts-receivable + purchase ledger.** Define the proposed credit policy and back-test: of the customers we *would* have approved, what % actually paid late or defaulted? Produces a real, observed loss curve on the exact population from a data-room request + an analyst-week.
- **Threshold → Continue:** retro-loss **<1%/cycle** → green-light Build; use the loss curve as the headline diligence proof in the Seed deck and the AG-warehouse confidence-builder.
- **→ Pivot:** **1–2%/cycle** → reshape to secured-only / tighter policy.
- **→ Kill:** **>2%/cycle** → the model is broken; stop before warehouse/license/build spend.

### Conditions to clear before Incubate (from diligence)
1. Local counsel opinion (no-license lane; not a "financial group"; AML scope).
2. Executed, transferable, multi-year channel + data-license agreements (survive change of control; data/IP assigned to the venture).
3. Clean borrower consent + data chain (GDPR-lite).
4. Model-validation plan with a real-repayment milestone + cold-start strategy.
5. Committed warehouse term sheet (covenant headroom) + ≥1 forward-flow buyer.
6. Concentration policy (cap construction-correlated + single-geography exposure; downturn stress test).

**Need-More-Evidence is a hold, not a stall** — it ships with the evidence ask above so the Board can act immediately (the retro-underwrite is also AG's own confidence-builder for anchoring the first warehouse).
